What is a p-value in simple terms?

Imagine you toss a fair coin 10 times. You expect heads to come up about 5 times. But what if it comes up 9 times? That seems strange, doesn't it?

A p-value is a number that tells you how "strange" or "unusual" your observation is, if in fact nothing strange was happening (i.e., if the coin were fair, or if the deaths were just a matter of chance).

    ¬† ¬†
  • If the p-value is small (e.g., less than 0.05 or 0.01): This means that your observation (9 heads out of 10) would be very, very unlikely if everything were "normal" (the coin is fair). It's as if the coin is screaming at you: "Hey, something's not right here! I'm probably not a fair coin!"
