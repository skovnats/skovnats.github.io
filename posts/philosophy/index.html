My Opinion on the Academic Environment

The academic environment is increasingly influenced by capitalism. The pursuit of truth and the solution of complex problems have given way to a focus on productivity metrics: citation counts, publications in prestigious journals, collaboration with famous scholars, and impressive CV entries.

The system encourages focusing on “low hanging fruits” (simple tasks), unjustified complication of texts through excessive formulas and terms to boost perceived contribution, as well as non-reproducible research, data manipulation, granting awards to “insiders,” and low quality of peer review due to professor overload. Honest research struggles to compete with methods that use unethical means. This is a systemic problem, though sometimes it is the fault of individuals.

The devaluation of academic publications and titles is occurring. We create a huge volume of materials that may only serve to distract talented people from truly important tasks.

I believe that people with exceptional abilities “have a duty” to use them for the benefit of society, helping the less gifted and “lighting the way” for others, rather than misusing their talents. This is comparable to a strong boxer using his superiority against beginners.

Nevertheless, I hope and believe that most scholars strive for truth and honest research. Otherwise, the situation would be much worse.
